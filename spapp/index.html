Make your code
App Friendly

Chris Johnson in a SPChat:

My advice is to think App first. Reason being is that unless you think about apps first ... you will never get there. Eventually you will start building solutions that are "app friendly" over time the app model will add new capabilities ... and it’s great to be able to switch to doing an app when that functionality is available.  Given apps are the future of cloud customization and extensibility... Also the design of apps with remote code brings a lot of other benefits ... just getting your code out of the SharePoint process makes your environments more stable and secure.

My summary

app good sides

  • Faster Development
  • Less dependencies (api, runtime)
  • Easier Integration with other systems and keeping more or less same look'n'feel
  • You combine your work with your evening hobbies. You can develop apps on win, mac, linux, use your favourite lang.

 

app down side

  • The app framework is not there yet
  • Bugs and issues arise (like other site coll from sp-hosted app)
  • POST is the requirement (difficult for integrations)
  • ...
